The Mindset

The Michelin Inspector Framework

A Michelin Inspector never calls ahead. They arrive unannounced, order the hardest dish on the menu, and evaluate against a fixed standard — not against how hard the kitchen worked or how much the chef cares. The restaurant either earns the star or it doesn't.

🍽️
You Are the Inspector
Not the chef. Not the builder. You have no emotional investment in whether this bot succeeds. Your job is to evaluate what is in front of you — nothing else. The standard is fixed. The bot either meets it or it doesn't.
🎯
Finding Failures Is the Goal
If you find a failure in Week 3, that is not a problem. That is the entire point of Week 3. Log it. Bring it to Week 4. The surgeon fixes what the inspector found. A clean test that misses real failures is the actual failure.
📋
No Test Scripts
A controlled step-by-step plan creates tunnel vision. The bot passes your rehearsed walkthrough and fails the first real user who doesn't follow your plan. Give testers a mindset — not a checklist.
🔄
Inspect → Surgery → Re-Inspect
Week 3 is the first inspection. Week 4 is Digital Brain Surgery — the surgeon fixes what the inspector found. Week 8 is the re-inspection. The goal is 3 stars by Week 8, not Week 3.
The Standard

The Three-Star Standard

Every bot is evaluated against the same fixed standard. Stars are earned through performance — not effort, not intention, not how long the build took.

⭐
1 Star — Functional
Does what it's supposed to do
Under ideal conditions, with clean inputs, the bot produces correct outputs. Basic functionality confirmed. Happy path passes.
⭐⭐
2 Stars — Resilient
Handles complexity gracefully
Edge cases managed. Ambiguous inputs handled. Bot asks for clarification instead of guessing. Scope boundaries hold under pressure.
⭐⭐⭐
3 Stars — Exceptional
Survives adversarial conditions
Creative destruction survived. Contradictions handled. Rules held under persistent pressure. Consistency confirmed across multiple runs and testers.
The goal is 3 stars by Week 8 — not Week 3. Week 3 tells you where you are. Week 4 closes the gap. A 1-star result in Week 3 is not a failing grade — it is a starting point with a clear direction.
How to Test

Three Testing Modes

Run all three modes — in any order — using real work examples. Not hypothetical scenarios. Not invented data. The bot must survive the inputs your real users will actually give it.

✅
Earn ⭐ 1 Star
Happy Path
Does it do what it's supposed to do?
  • 1Use real work data — not hypothetical scenarios
  • 2Run the same input 2–3 times and check for consistency
  • 3Vary the phrasing — same meaning, different words
  • 4Trace every answer back to the source — did it fabricate anything?
  • 5Check usability — can you use the output directly, or does it need cleanup?
⚠️
Earn ⭐⭐ 2 Stars
Edge Cases
Does it hold at the boundaries?
  • 1Give it missing fields or partial information
  • 2Give it unclear or conflicting instructions — which does it trust?
  • 3Give it data structured differently than expected
  • 4When it doesn't know something, does it say so — or fill the blank?
  • 5Set a rule at the start. At message 15, does it still hold?
💥
Earn ⭐⭐⭐ 3 Stars
Creative Destruction
Does it break under pressure?
  • 1Give it contradictory information — which does it trust?
  • 2Ask something slightly outside scope — does it hold the boundary or drift?
  • 3Push it to make something up — does it resist or comply?
  • 4Does it announce it will do something and then not do it? Does it loop without resolving?
  • 5Persistently pressure it to break a rule. Does it hold or eventually give in?
The Inspector's Code

Three Rules Every Tester Follows

These are not suggestions. They are the difference between testing that produces usable data and testing that produces false confidence.

01
The Inspector's Rule
A Michelin Inspector never gives a restaurant a pass because the chef tried hard. A tester never gives a bot a pass because the builder worked hard. The standard is fixed. Evaluate what is in front of you — not the effort behind it.
02
The Anti-Script Rule
Do not write a test script. A controlled step-by-step plan — "Step 1 do this, expect this" — tells users exactly what to do. This creates tunnel vision. The bot passes your rehearsed walkthrough and fails the first real user who doesn't follow your plan. Real users don't read your test script.
03
The Ownership Detachment Rule
You helped build this bot. You want it to succeed. That instinct will make you a bad inspector. The Michelin Inspector has never set foot in that kitchen. They have zero emotional investment in the outcome. When you sit down to test: you did not build this bot. You are evaluating it.
Log everything. One row per issue in your shared testing log. Paste the actual input you gave and the actual output you received. A comment without an example is an opinion. An example with a comment is a diagnosis. Bring diagnoses to Week 4 — not opinions.
What Clean Structured Data Looks Like

Week 4 is surgery. Surgery requires a patient chart — not a vague complaint. Every test entry must have all five fields. Incomplete rows are not usable data.

1
Like / Dislike
One word. Your gut reaction to the output. This is the good/bad distinction — before any analysis.
2
Input & Output
Paste exactly what you typed or uploaded. Paste exactly what the bot returned. Word for word — no paraphrasing. This is the evidence.
3
Why
One sentence. What made it good or bad? A comment without a reason is an opinion. A reason without evidence is a guess. Both together is a diagnosis.
4
Notes — How You'd Like It Done
If you know what correct looks like — describe it. Like ordering a steak: medium rare, no sauce, side of fries. Be specific. The surgeon needs the order, not just the complaint. Leave blank if unsure — but if you know, say it.
🎲
Do Not Pass GO
In Monopoly, you don't collect $200 if you don't pass GO. Same rule here. Week 3 testing must be fully documented — real inputs, real outputs, one row per issue — before anyone moves to Week 4. Incomplete testing means the improvement session has nothing to work with. No data, no surgery.
